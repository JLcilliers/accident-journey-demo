Stage 1

Trigger & Awareness

Discovery

The journey starts in the first 24–72 hours after a crash, when the user is confused, stressed, and told they'll "need the police report."

  • They search queries like "how to get my accident report in Denver" or "free accident report lookup".
  • Real-time accident pages and location-specific SEO content we own show up in organic results.
  • In some cases they arrive via QR codes or links from tow trucks, insurers, or existing PI partners.
  • Our goal: capture this intent before they talk to competing firms or get lost in government bureaucracy.

Stage 2

Landing / Hero Experience

Trust builder

This page reassures the user that the process is simple and legitimate, then funnels them into the guided wizard.

  • Clear promise: "Find your official accident report in minutes" with a simple 3-step strip (search → confirm → receive).
  • Localized explanation of how reports work in their state and typical timelines (e.g., 3–7 days).
  • Trust markers: "Secure", "No upfront cost", "We work with official public records".
  • One dominant CTA: "Start free accident report search", plus an optional chat-style AI helper.

Stage 3

Accident Search Wizard

Input

A short, forgiving form that gathers just enough information to match the incident against feeds and public records.

  • Fields: state (auto-detected), city/county, date of accident, incident type, closest street/intersection.
  • Copy reassures them that approximate info is okay – the system can still work with "around last Sunday" or "near I-25".
  • Under the hood (MVP+), this payload is used to query incident feeds, social data, and agency APIs where available.
  • All inputs are stored in structured form for later SEO + analytics ("crash heatmaps", "dangerous intersections").

Stage 4

Incident Matching

Logic

Here we translate the inputs into specific candidate crashes and let the user confirm whether we've found the right one.

  • We show 1–3 likely incidents (time, road, direction, vehicles involved, high-level severity).
  • The user chooses "Yes, that's mine" or "None of these / I'm not sure" – but the flow never hard-stops.
  • On "not sure", we keep a fuzzy link to the closest incident while still moving them forward.
  • Confirmed incidents get a unique ID we can use for severity scoring, routing priority, and reporting.

Stage 5

Role & Contact Capture

Lead signal

This is where the funnel becomes a qualified lead flow. We find out who they are and whether they're a fit for a sponsoring PI firm.

  • Ask their role (driver, passenger, pedestrian/cyclist, family member) and injury status (yes / maybe / no).
  • Capture whether insurance or a lawyer has contacted them and whether they're already represented.
  • Collect name, email, mobile number, preferred contact method, and explicit consent to be contacted.
  • These answers determine a lead score and whether they qualify for immediate sponsor routing in their market.

Stage 6

Report Status & Delivery

Value moment

Regardless of backend limitations, this screen has to feel like the payoff: the user understands exactly where things stand.

  • If APIs allow, we fetch the report and present a summary plus a link to receive the full PDF via email/SMS.
  • If the report is not yet available, we show a realistic local timeline and offer to notify them instantly when it's posted.
  • In constrained jurisdictions, we generate clear, personalized instructions on how to request the report themselves.
  • This is the moment where they feel the platform "did the hard work" of decoding the bureaucracy for them.

Stage 7

Legal Routing & Long-term Value

Output

Hot leads get routed to sponsors; anonymised data becomes the backbone of our "AI-first SEO agency" story.

  • If injured, unrepresented, and in a covered market, users see a strong offer for a free consult with a local PI firm.
  • Leads are pushed into sponsor CRMs/intake tools with full context (incident, injury, contact channel preferences).
  • Automated email/SMS flows handle report updates, education, and low-pressure reactivation.
  • Aggregated data powers dashboards and content around crash trends, dangerous intersections, and market coverage for pitches.
