High-level stages

These seven stages map the full experience from first Google search after an accident through to legal routing and long-term data value.

1

Trigger & Awareness

Post-accident "how do I get the report?" moment

SEO pages, real-time accident content, or referrals pull them in.

What's happening here?

  • User has just had an accident or is helping someone who did (driver, passenger, family member).
  • They Google things like "how to get my accident report in Denver" or "free police report lookup".
  • They land on our geo-specific landing page or an incident-specific page driven by real-time monitoring.
2

Landing / Hero Experience

Clear promise: "Find your accident report in minutes"

Reassurance, social proof, and a single CTA: "Find my report".

What's happening here?

  • Page explains the 3-step flow: search → confirm incident → get report/guide.
  • Trust elements: "secure", "no upfront cost", jurisdiction-specific explanation of how reports work.
  • Main CTA kicks off the guided wizard; optional AI assistant for natural-language input ("I crashed on I-25 yesterday…").
3

Accident Search Wizard

Collects basics: state, city, date, type, intersection

Feels guided and forgiving, even when inputs are fuzzy.

What's happening here?

  • Minimal fields first: state, city/county, date, type of incident, closest street/intersection.
  • Smart defaults (e.g., pre-fill state/city from IP), plus messaging that approximate info is okay.
  • Backend/AI in the real MVP would use this to query incident feeds and agency data for potential matches.
4

Incident Matching

Show likely accidents and ask "Does this look like yours?"

Narrows to the specific crash, or falls back gracefully.

What's happening here?

  • Display 1–3 candidate incidents with time, rough location, and number of vehicles.
  • User picks "yes, that's mine" or "none of these / not sure".
  • On "not sure", wizard asks for a bit more info (time-of-day, vehicle type) but still progresses, never dead-ends.
5

Role & Contact Capture

Critical qualification step for lead routing

Who are they (driver/passenger/etc.), were they injured, and how can we reach them?

What's happening here?

  • Ask user's role (driver, passenger, pedestrian, family member) and injury status (yes / maybe / no).
  • Ask whether insurance or a lawyer has contacted them yet, plus if they already have a lawyer.
  • Capture name, email, phone, consent for contact. These fields power routing, prioritisation, and later reporting to clients.
6

Report Status & Delivery

Shows "instant", "not yet available", or "guide-only"

This is where the perceived value is delivered and trust is won.

What's happening here?

  • If auto-retrieval is possible, show "Good news, your report is available" with a download/email option.
  • If it's not ready yet, show a realistic timeline and offer to notify them as soon as it is.
  • In locked-down jurisdictions, provide a personalised step-by-step guide (agency, forms, fees) so the tool still feels valuable.
7

Legal Routing & Long-term Value

Connect high-value users to sponsors and build SEO assets

Qualification signals + location drive routing to PI partners.

What's happening here?

  • If they're injured, in a supported market, and unrepresented, we offer a sponsor-attorney call, callback, or consult.
  • Regardless of lead status, we send follow-up emails/SMS for report availability, education, and reactivation.
  • Anonymised incident and funnel data feed dashboards and SEO content: "dangerous intersections", "local crash trends", and authority-building assets for pitches.
