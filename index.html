High-level stages

We map the experience from the user's first "how do I get my police report?" search, through guided accident identification, report delivery, and finally routing qualified leads to sponsoring PI firms.

1

Trigger & Awareness

First post-accident "I need my report" moment

Stage

SEO content, real-time accident pages, and referrals funnel the user into our experience.

What happens:

  • User has recently been in a crash (or is helping a family member) and is told they'll need an official report.
  • They search things like "get my accident report Denver" or tap a link from a tow truck, insurer, or law firm.
  • They land on a geo-specific or incident-specific page we own, powered by AI-generated content and monitoring feeds.
2

Landing / Hero Experience

Clear promise & single CTA: "Find my report"

Trust

The page calms them down, explains the process, and pushes them into the guided search wizard.

What happens:

  • Hero copy: "Find your official accident report in minutes" with a breakdown of the 3–step flow.
  • Trust badges & local nuance ("In Colorado, reports are usually ready in 3–7 days…").
  • Main CTA starts the flow; optional AI assistant lets them describe the crash in plain language.
3

Accident Search Wizard

Collects just enough data to match incidents

Input

State, city, date, type, and rough location are gathered in a forgiving, mobile-friendly wizard.

What happens:

  • Form starts with auto-detected state/city, plus date picker and incident type.
  • An open text "closest street / intersection" field captures messy human input without friction.
  • In production, this payload is sent to services scraping public feeds and agency logs to find likely matches.
4

Incident Matching

"Does this look like your accident?" list

Logic

The system proposes one or more likely crashes, but never dead-ends if there's no perfect match.

What happens:

  • Cards show time, road, direction, and vehicles involved for 1–3 likely incidents.
  • User selects "this is mine" or "none of these / not sure"; we still proceed on "not sure" with a slightly fuzzier link.
  • Matched incident becomes a key for later analytics and severity scoring.
5

Role & Contact Capture

Critical qualification step for routing

Lead

We learn who they are, whether they're hurt, and if they're already represented.

What happens:

  • Fields: role (driver, passenger, pedestrian, family), injury status, insurance contact, and "do you already have a lawyer?"
  • Personal info: name, email, phone, preferred contact method, and explicit consent for outreach.
  • These inputs produce a clear lead score and determine if the user is eligible for immediate sponsor connection.
6

Report Status & Delivery

Instant, pending, or guide-only state

Value

This screen pays off the promise and explains next steps clearly, even when APIs can't fetch the report.

What happens:

  • If possible, we fetch the report instantly and let them view a summary and request the full PDF by email/SMS.
  • If not yet ready, we show local timelines, provide a pre-report summary, and commit to notifying them as soon as it drops.
  • In manual jurisdictions, we generate a personalised "how to request your report in [City]" guide, preserving perceived value.
7

Legal Routing & Long-term Value

Connect qualified leads & build data assets

Output

Hot leads go to sponsoring firms; anonymised data feeds SEO, dashboards, and pitch material.

What happens:

  • If the user is injured, unrepresented, and in a covered market, they're offered an immediate call or callback from a sponsor.
  • Email/SMS flows handle report updates, education, and reactivation while staying compliance-friendly.
  • Aggregate trends (dangerous intersections, volume by weekday, etc.) become content and proof that we already own demand in a market when pitching firms.
